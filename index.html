About

I approach problems the same way I approached optimization in competitive gaming: treat everything as a system. Identify variables. Test hypotheses. Eliminate inefficiencies. Then do it again, faster.

"In a college programming class, we were tasked with writing a program to solve Tower of Hanoi. While my classmates began coding the full recursive solution, I recognized that the minimum moves follow a predictable mathematical pattern: 2n - 1. I implemented the formula in minutes and delivered exactly what was asked — nothing more, nothing less."

My professor was impressed not by complexity, but by precision. I solved the problem by understanding it first.

I've achieved Top 0.1% global ranking across multiple concurrent performance metrics — not once, but twice. First cycle: reached target 70% of the way through the season. Second cycle: achieved the same result within the first 25% of the season — a 200% improvement in execution speed. This wasn't luck; it was systematic optimization, validated through repetition.
