About

I'm an analyst and problem-solver with a background in Game Localization QA for an MNC, where I evaluated content for accuracy, consistency, and cultural appropriateness. This was fully remote work — collaborating across time zones, managing tasks independently, and delivering quality results for international stakeholders. This work trained me to notice what others overlook — and to articulate clear, actionable solutions.

I approach problems the same way I approached optimization in competitive gaming: treat everything as a system. Identify variables. Test hypotheses. Eliminate inefficiencies. Then do it again, faster.

In a college class, we were tasked with writing a program to solve the Tower of Hanoi — specifically, to return the minimum number of moves required for any given number of discs. While my classmates began coding the full recursive solution, I recognized that the minimum moves follow a predictable mathematical pattern: 2ⁿ - 1. I implemented the formula in minutes and delivered exactly what was asked — nothing more, nothing less.

My professor was impressed not by complexity, but by precision. I solved the problem by understanding it first.

I thrive on novelty — which is why I'm open to multiple industries. Give me data and a problem, and I'll figure out the rest.

I work best when given a clear objective — like a school project. Tell me what success looks like, and I'll figure out the path. I don't need micromanaging. I need trust and a problem to solve.

This same mindset led to achieving Top 0.1% global ranking across multiple concurrent performance metrics — not once, but twice, with the second execution achieved in under 3 weeks — nearly 3x faster than the first. (See Case Study.)

So whether it's content review, competitive performance, or breaking down a problem — I just like figuring out how things work and making them better. No drama. No overcomplicating. Just clear thinking and honest effort.

If you have a system that could run more smoothly — or a problem you're not sure how to approach — I'd love to hear about it.
